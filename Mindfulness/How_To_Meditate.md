# How to Meditate: A Practical Guide 🧘‍♂️

Welcome to your guide to meditation. Here you will find practical tips to start and manage your daily practice.

## 🪑 1. The Posture
You don't need to be a contortionist. Stability and comfort are key:
- **Chair:** Feet flat on the floor, back straight but not rigid (don't lean back if possible).
- **Cushion (Zafu):** Sitting cross-legged, with your pelvis slightly higher than your knees.
- **Hands:** Resting on your knees or in your lap.

## 👃 2. The Focus: The Breath
Your breath is your anchor. When your mind wanders (and it will!), gently return here:
- Observe the air entering and leaving your nostrils.
- Feel the rising and falling of your abdomen.
- Do not force your breath; let it be natural.

## 🧠 3. Managing Thoughts
Meditation is **not** about emptying your mind, but about noticing when you are thinking:
1. **Notice:** "Ah, I'm thinking about the grocery list."
2. **Label:** Give it a quick name (e.g., "thought", "project", "emotion").
3. **Return:** Return to the breath without judging yourself. Every time you return, you are "training the muscle" of attention.

## ⏰ 4. Tips for the 4 Moments of the Day

### 🌅 Morning (Energizing)
- **Focus:** Clarity and intention.
- **Tip:** Meditate as soon as you wake up, before looking at your phone. Set an intention for the day (e.g., "Today I will be patient").

### ☀️ Post-Lunch (Reset)
- **Focus:** Recharging.
- **Tip:** Ideal for disconnecting from work. Even just 5 minutes can prevent afternoon mental fatigue.

### 🌆 Evening (Decompression)
- **Focus:** Letting go.
- **Tip:** Visualize the day closing. Let go of any stress or frustrations.

### 🌙 Pre-Sleep (Relaxation)
- **Focus:** Body Scan or Gratitude.
- **Tip:** Meditate even while lying in bed. Mentally review 3 things you are grateful for today.

---
> [!IMPORTANT]
> Meditation is a process, not a performance. There will be "calm" days and "chaotic" days. Both are valid.
