# Smoking Cessation Roadmap 🚬🚫

This roadmap applies the general framework specifically to tobacco use. The focus is on progressive reduction to minimize withdrawal symptoms and change your identity from "smoker" to "non-smoker."

## 🏁 Phase 1: Awareness (Week 1)
**Goal:** Know your enemy.
- **Action:** Smoke as usual, but record **every single cigarette** in your weekly journal.
- **Focus:** Write the trigger for each cigarette (e.g., "finished eating", "waiting for bus", "work stress").

## 📈 Phase 2: Eliminating "Easy" Cigarettes (Week 2-3)
**Goal:** 20-30% reduction.
- **Action:** Identify the least important cigarettes (those you smoke out of boredom or habit) and eliminate them.
- **Strategy:** Use a substitute (water, gum, breath) for those specific occasions.

## 🔄 Phase 3: Time Control (Week 4-5)
**Goal:** Break temporal associations.
- **Action:** Set time windows. Delay the first cigarette of the day by at least 1 hour from waking up.
- **Strategy:** Never smoke in a specific location again (e.g., in the car or on the balcony).

## 🏆 Phase 4: Towards "Quit Day" (Week 6+)
**Goal:** Consolidation and closure.
- **Action:** Reduce to a minimum (e.g., 2-3 cigarettes per day). Set a precise date for absolute zero.
- **Strategy:** Increase meditation sessions and physical activity to manage dopamine.

---
> [!TIP]
> Every cigarette **not smoked** is a victory for your lungs and your wallet. Watch the weekly total go down!
