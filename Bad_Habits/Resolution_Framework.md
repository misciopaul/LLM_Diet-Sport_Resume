# Habit Resolution Framework 🚫

This framework is based on behavioral change science (The Habit Loop) to help you identify, reduce, and eventually eliminate unwanted behaviors.

## 🔄 1. Understanding the Habit Loop
Every habit consists of three elements:
1. **Cue (Trigger):** The stimulus that triggers the behavior (e.g., stress, boredom, coffee, a specific time).
2. **Routine:** The behavior itself (e.g., smoking a cigarette).
3. **Reward:** The perceived benefit (e.g., relaxation, break, stimulation).

## 🛠 2. Resolution Strategies

### A. Trigger Identification
For the first week, don't try to quit. Simply note **when** and **why** you give in to the habit. Use the "Trigger" column in your journal.

### B. Gradual Reduction (Tapering)
Instead of quitting "Cold Turkey," reduce the weekly quantity.
- **Set a maximum limit:** Establish how many times a day is allowed.
- **Delay:** When you feel the urge, wait 5-10 minutes before acting. Often, the urgency subsides.

### C. Habit Substitution
It's difficult to eliminate a routine without replacing the reward. Find a healthy substitute for the same cue:
- **Cue: Stress** -> **Old Routine: Smoking** -> **New Routine: 2 min of deep breathing.**
- **Cue: Boredom** -> **Old Routine: Social Media** -> **New Routine: Read 2 pages of a book.**

## 📈 3. Progress Monitoring
Awareness is key. Tracking every single occurrence makes you aware of the automatic behavior, breaking the cycle.

---
> [!IMPORTANT]
> A "slip-up" is not a failure. It is data. Analyze what triggered it and resume your plan the next moment.
